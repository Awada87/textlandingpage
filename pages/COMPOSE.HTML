Behavioural Pattern of Chameleon

Behavior: Panther chameleons are diurnal, solitary and territorial. The home ranges of males are usually larger than those of females. Males defend their territory by chasing and/or displaying. During courtship or when defending territory, head bobbing, drastic color changes and inflation of the body may occur.A chameleon's behavioral adaptation is colour change. Chameleons change their colors to represent mood. For example, a chameleon may change red or red orange if it feels angry or territorial. Also chameleons change color to match their environment.
